# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary audience:** Upper-middle and upper-class homeowners in Nepal (primarily Kathmandu Valley) who are furnishing a new home, redecorating, or purchasing premium furniture. They are house owners, typically browsing on mobile, arriving from Meta ads or WhatsApp conversations. They want to see the full catalog, compare materials, and confirm quality before visiting the Gyaneshwor showroom or ordering via WhatsApp.

**Secondary audiences:**
- Interior designers and architects sourcing for client projects
- Office and corporate buyers (chairs, desks, bulk orders)
- Cafe, restaurant, and hotel owners (commercial furniture)
- NRNs (Non-Resident Nepalis) furnishing homes remotely

**Job to be done:** Browse premium furniture with confidence, understand material quality and pricing, and either purchase directly (under NPR 30,000 via COD/WhatsApp) or build enough trust to visit the showroom for high-value items. The website must bridge the gap between seeing an ad and making a purchase decision -- answering the "why is this worth the price?" question that current sales channels fail to communicate.

## Product Purpose

AB Furniture and Furnishing is a 20-year-old premium furniture retailer in Gyaneshwor, Kathmandu. The website serves as the brand's digital showroom and primary sales channel, replacing the current ad-to-WhatsApp-only funnel with a full product catalog experience.

**What it does:**
- Showcases 70+ products across furniture (sofas, tables, chairs, beds, storage) and furnishing (curtains, carpets, wallpapers, flooring) with MRP pricing in NPR
- Enables a hybrid e-commerce flow: products display with prices, "Buy" actions lead to WhatsApp with pre-filled product details
- Builds brand credibility through material storytelling, warranty transparency, and customization options
- Drives foot traffic to the physical showroom for high-value purchases

**Success looks like:**
- Customers understand the value proposition before asking "why so expensive?"
- WhatsApp inquiry volume increases with higher-intent, better-informed leads
- Showroom visits increase from website-sourced customers
- The client can self-manage products, prices, banners, and blog content via the admin panel

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Premium, Warm, Trustworthy

**Voice & tone:**
- Premium and luxurious without being cold or exclusionary -- think a knowledgeable showroom host, not a luxury fashion brand
- Warm and family-oriented, reflecting the Nepali cultural context of furnishing a home as a meaningful life milestone
- Professional and confident about quality -- direct about materials, warranties, and craftsmanship without overselling
- Language is English with occasional Nepenglish (Nepali-English mix) for cultural resonance

**Emotional goals:** The interface should make visitors feel they are browsing a curated, high-quality collection -- like walking into a well-lit showroom. Confidence in quality, warmth of hospitality, and the quiet authority of two decades of expertise.

## Anti-references

- **Generic dropshipping sites** -- No cheap-looking product grids with cluttered information, stock badges, or urgency countdown timers. AB Furniture is not Daraz or a marketplace.
- **Over-animated SaaS landing pages** -- No parallax overkill, floating particles, or tech-startup aesthetics. This is furniture, not software.
- **Cold minimalism without substance** -- Avoid empty white space that says nothing. Every section should communicate craftsmanship, material quality, or trust.
- **Cluttered Indian e-commerce** -- While WoodenStreet is a layout reference, avoid their information density, popup overload, and visual noise. Cleaner, more editorial.
- **Sites that hide pricing** -- Transparency builds trust. Show MRP prominently. For exclusive items, show "Starting from NPR X" -- never hide the price behind a contact form.

## Design Principles

1. **Material honesty** -- Let the wood, marble, and fabric speak. Photography and texture are the primary communication tools. Design decisions should amplify the physical quality of the products, not compete with them.

2. **Earned trust, not claimed** -- Build credibility through specifics (20 years, 5-year warranty, Sheesham Wood), not generic superlatives. Every trust signal should be verifiable or concrete.

3. **Warmth at premium** -- Premium does not mean cold. The aesthetic blends luxury with the warmth of a Nepali home -- wood tones, warm lighting, family-oriented context. The site should feel like an invitation, not a velvet rope.

4. **Mobile-first clarity** -- 70%+ of traffic comes from Meta ads on mobile. Every design decision must work on a phone screen first. Information hierarchy, touch targets, and scroll pacing are non-negotiable.

5. **Show the value, close on WhatsApp** -- The website's job is to build confidence and communicate value. The conversion happens on WhatsApp. Every page should have a clear, low-friction path to a WhatsApp conversation with product context pre-filled.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA compliance** as baseline target
- Ensure sufficient contrast ratios across the dark (charcoal) and light (ivory) surface modes -- the brand palette of orange on dark backgrounds must meet 4.5:1 for text
- All product images must have descriptive alt text (product name, material, key feature)
- Keyboard navigation must work for the full shop browsing flow (category filters, product cards, WhatsApp CTAs)
- Touch targets minimum 44x44px for mobile -- critical for WhatsApp buttons and product card interactions
- Support `prefers-reduced-motion`: disable scroll-reveal animations, hero zoom, and carousel auto-play
- NPR price formatting must use `tabular-nums` for alignment and screen reader compatibility
- Form inputs (inquiry form, search) must have visible focus states and proper label associations
